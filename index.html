About Us

Welcome to MLW Geospatial technical Support Team, an informal group of geospatial professionals affliated with the Malawi Liverpool Wellcome Programme. Our team works to provide support to researchers interested in using the techniques in geospatial sciences in their work...

Our Mission

Our mission is to strenghten the capacity of Malawi to use both basic and cutting-edge technologies developed in the field of 'geography', 'computer science' and 'mathematics' to study diseases and save lives.

Why a Geospatial Technical team?

The use of mapping science in disease is as old as the field of epideomology. Actually, the father of Epideomology, Dr John Snow used mapping science to understand how the spread of cholera. The science of studying how diseases spread have become more established that there is a special niche called 'spatial epideomology'. Not only that mapping sciences have advanced with now there has been growing use of cuttingedge technology such as Machine Learning, drone imaging, High-Perfomance Computing among others. These are tools that cannot only help to address questions that researchers seek to address but also support teams that support research work (community engagement, operations and policy). However, these teams do not always have a GIS professionals. Therefore, we have started our team to fill the gap plus assist in training of Malawian geospatial professionals through coaching, mentorship and in-house skill development and collaborations.
